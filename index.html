Project

1) Experimental Investigation and Performance Analysis of Heat Pipe


A heat pipe is a two phase heat transfer device that combines the principles of both thermal conductivity and phase transition to effectively transfer heat between three sections. We fabricated a copper heat pipe setup with three sections(Evaporator, Adiabatic and Condenser sections).The cylindrical heat pipe was tested with different fluid volumes and was tested for three different heat inputs and three different flow rates of the condenser liquid with and without PCM at different fill ratio of deionized water as it's working fluid and the temperature is noted using Arduino based temperature data logger.

Amoung these alterations, an optimum value of heat input, flow rate and fill volume based upon performance, which provides less resistance, better thermal conductivity, overall heat transfer coefficient, thermal resistance and performance were calculated and studied. The efficient parameter setup is selected based on the thermal resistance for heat flow calculated. The lowest thermal resistance is considered to be the efficient parameter.

2) Simulation of Cost Optimal 3d Environment Mapping Using Dual Axially Rotating Lidar Sensors


LIDAR mapping is widely accepted over other mapping systems like RADAR, Camera etc. due to its 360 degree long distance (100 to 300 m) mapping ability and unlike Camera systems it can work in night time without blind spot issues. An innovative mechanical system containing dual axially rotating wheels will make use of few sensors to reach most of the 3D space efficiently. The sensors moves along varying helical passages as it rotates due to difference in speeds of the rotating wheels. When the data from sensors is combined, we obtain a mesh like structure of the environment. The mesh can be refined by varying the speed, angle of inclined wheel and alignment of the sensors around the inclined wheel thus achieving substantial information using minimum number of sensor units. SciLab is used to simulate the mathematical model of the system.

5) Designing and fabrication of a model usign 3D printer


3D printer is form of additive manufacturing technology where a three dimensional object is created by laying down successive layers of material. It is known as rapid prototyping. The filament material used here is called as Polylactic Acid(PLA). First, the 3d model is designed using CATIA V5 and saved in stereolithography(STL) format before slicing.

The model is sliced and G-Code is generated based on factors like layer height, speed, infill density. The adhesion and support are also mentioned based on the design and printer capacity. The generated G-Code is imported into 3d printer.

paper presentation

IEEE GCAT


Presented a paper on Resilience optimization of octocopter drone using two stage thrusters and thrust vector locking in IEEE Global Conference for Advancement in Technology. (Vigneshwar , Ranjit Roshan and Noufal.)

Octocopters use fault handling programs to stabilize and land safely in case of a motor failure. This paper is about ,how the motors can be optimally placed and thrust vectoring can be used, to stabilize and resume normal operation in case of motor failure or propeller breakage.

By having such arrangement we can achieve translation without actual rotation. Similarly we can achieve yaw motion without changing the speed of the thrusters. Aligning the thrusters at the desired angle helps to translation along the respective direction.
