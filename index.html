Project

1) Experimental Investigation and Performance Analysis of Heat Pipe


  • Heat pipe is a two phase heat transfer device that combines the principles of both thermal conductivity and phase transition to effectively transfer heat between three sections.
  • We fabricated a copper heat pipe setup with three sections(Evaporator, Adiabatic and Condenser sections).
  • The cylindrical heat pipe was tested with different fluid volumes and was tested for three different heat inputs and three different flow rates of the condenser liquid with and without PCM at different fill ratio of deionized water as it's working fluid and the temperature is noted using Arduino based temperature data logger.

  • Amoung these alterations, an optimum value of heat input, flow rate and fill volume based upon performance, which provides less resistance, better thermal conductivity, overall heat transfer coefficient, thermal resistance and performance were calculated and studied.
  • Efficient parameter setup is selected based on the thermal resistance for heat flow calculated. The lowest thermal resistance is considered to be the efficient parameter.


2) Simulation of Cost Optimal 3d Environment Mapping Using Dual Axially Rotating Lidar Sensors


  • LIDAR mapping is widely accepted over other mapping systems like RADAR, Camera etc. Due to its 360 degree long distance (100 to 300 m) mapping ability and unlike Camera systems it can work in night time without blind spot issues.
  • An innovative mechanical system containing dual axially rotating wheels will make use of few sensors to reach most of the 3D space efficiently.
  • The sensors moves along varying helical passages as it rotates due to difference in speeds of the rotating wheels. When the data from sensors is combined, we obtain a mesh like structure of the environment.
  • The mesh can be refined by varying the speed, angle of inclined wheel and alignment of the sensors around the inclined wheel thus achieving substantial information using minimum number of sensor units. SciLab is used to simulate the mathematical model of the system.

Research intern

  • Worked on analysis of aerofoil for generating high lift when wing flap is introduced. The aerodynamic efficiency of the wing is Increased by adding a feature below the wing surface.
  • Analysis was conducted for various Parameters with different feature designs, angle of attack, and range of velocities. For each data, Performing CFD simulation using Ansys Fluent and then the parameter of the wing with the maximum efficiency (Lift-Drag ratio) is selected.
  • Input parameters are:
    1. Wing angle - Angle of attack (Degrees)
    2. Inner Flap Angle - alpha (Degrees)
    3. Outer Flap Angle - Beta (Degrees)
  • Output parameters are:
    1. Coefficient of lift (Cl) / coefficient of drag (Cd)
    2. Lift force (Fl) / drag force (Fd)
  • The tests were conducted for various values of input parameters and it was clearly visible that the wing with feature flaps has higher aerodynamic efficiency done the wings with simple flaps
